Animal Endangerment

Animal endangerment has been a serious concern since the dreadful introduction of plastic. More than 700 species are affected by its repercussions, and millions of organisms die at its clutch every year. Plastic poses a threat to the entirety of life on our planet, as its suffocating properties end up in every speciesâ€™ meal.

Oceanic creatures-

As plastics, which are broken into smaller, more hazardous pieces known as microplastics, various deep sea creatures are at potential risk.

Such plastic can be discovered at shorelines, sedimented at seafloors, floating at surface level (if not broken down completely)or in water columns.

The vibrant coloured nature of our plastic products attract fish that exist in such levels.

Most plastic may be mistaken as plankton (a diverse collection of organisms at the base of the food chain which are a major source for both small and large aquatic organisms) This may lead to the ingestion or suffocation of the organisms after consumption of such plastic.

As plankton is at the base of the food chain, it being mistaken can get plastic a long way up the food chain, and even to our plates.

Microplastic consumption causes dysfunction of internal organs.

Furthermore, plastic consumption provides the creatures with an illusion of being full, which leads to starvation. Such cases lead to death.

Microplastics can absorb oceanic polluters with ease, increasing the toxic effect on marine life.

Consumption of such pollution infused plastics can tamper with the health of aquatic creatures post reproduction.

Researchers have discovered that micro plastic, being extremely viable for consumption, has reached even the polyps in underwater corals.

Land creatures-

Land creatures also receive a substantial level of effect from the environmental degradation of plastic.

Plastic consumption by land animals can cause blockages in internal organs (especially the intestine), leading to fatalities

Plastic wrapped around the limbs of such organisms can lead to higher vulnerability to predators due to decreased agility

Creatures such as snakes and dogs are often found with their heads unable to move in improperly disposed plastic containers (often of food). This can potentially lead to suffocation.

Antlers and feet of animals are often affected by plastic entanglement
